A Day in the Life of a PTC

A Day in the Life of a PTC
Stepping into the role of a Partner Technical Consultant (PTC) for Azure Infrastructure feels like being at the heart of an intricate and dynamic dance, where every move counts and every day brings a new challenge to solve. My day begins with the sun barely up, coffee steaming beside me, and my operational boards lighting up my screen. Each tile on the board tells a story—a case waiting to be solved, a partner looking for guidance, and a roadmap for the day ahead. It’s a moment of anticipation, a chance to plan and dive into the day’s challenges with clarity.

Oh, it’s morning already!
An exciting day ahead. The operational boards highlight my priorities, pending cases, and where my focus needs to be for the day. Emails come next, serving as reminders of follow-ups and partner commitments. On a good day, I get to engage in three partner consultations. Each session is an opportunity to solve real-world problems, share knowledge, and witness the "aha" moments when solutions click for the partners. These interactions drive tangible results and build lasting relationships.

First steps—Let’s tackle priorities
I start by setting up a call with a partner who needs guidance on moving NetApp files from one tenant to another. With 30 minutes to spare before the call, I shoot off an email and prep myself for the discussion. While preparing, I recall a request from my colleague in Modern Work: a partner that has already mastered CoPilot is now exploring Azure AI. I schedule an hour-long session for later, ensuring a buffer for unexpected overruns.

The first call—NetApp files and a smooth start
The NetApp session goes smoothly. As the partner walks me through their setup, I ask, “Have you considered automating some of these workflows to streamline the migration?” Their eyes light up as we dive into possibilities. By the end of the session, we’ve addressed the migration challenge and even explored Sentinel—a topic they’re keen on developing further. I make a note to follow up with an enablement session on security.

Unexpected challenges—Here comes licensing
Out of the blue, a licensing case emerges. While it’s not in my scope, I know the partner needs help. I set up a call to guide them toward the right resources, ensuring no roadblocks in their journey. The satisfaction of turning a potential deadlock into a win is always worth the effort.

Moments of joy—Balancing work and family
After handling the licensing case, I address Azure AI and Modern Work needs. The partner’s progress feels like a shared win. Post-call, I take a brief pause to play with my daughter. Her laughter rejuvenates me, reminding me of the balance I strive to maintain between work and personal life.

Time to dive deep—SAP HANA and critical research
With two hours before my next meeting, I delve into a SAP HANA Disaster Recovery case. The partner’s detailed documentation saves me time, allowing me to focus on meaningful research. Challenges like these underscore the importance of clarity in requirements—the fewer assumptions, the better the outcomes.

Learning and growing—Community calls
After lunch and a few CoPilot-assisted tasks, I join a community call. These sessions are treasure troves of insights, equipping me with fresh ideas to help partners build, sell, and grow. Every concept shared here feels like a new tool in my PTC toolkit.

A complex puzzle—Financial and government entity
A financial and government entity case lands on my desk next. With six solution areas involved, it’s an all-hands-on-deck scenario. I initiate discussions with colleagues, knowing CAF Landing Zones and Azure Stack HCI will be pivotal. Collaboration is the key to solving such intricate puzzles.

The final stretch—Reflections and wrap-ups
As the day winds down, my security PTC sends a detailed Sentinel report, asking for a follow-up call. The gaps identified are critical, and we schedule a session for tomorrow. Wrapping up learnings, feedback sessions, and a team meeting, I reflect on the magic of the day. It’s not just about solving problems or completing tasks; it’s about the growth—mine, my partners’, and my team’s.

Every day is an adventure
Each challenge tackled, insight shared, and success achieved weaves into a story of continuous learning and collaboration that makes every moment worthwhile. Being a PTC is not just a job; it’s a journey of impact and growth. And at the heart of it all, the belief that every problem has a solution or a workaround drives us to achieve more.
